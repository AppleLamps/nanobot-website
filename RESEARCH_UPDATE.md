# Nancy Guthrie Missing Person Case - Research Update

**Last Updated:** February 7, 2026  
**Case Status:** ACTIVE - Missing Person / Suspected Abduction  
**FBI Reward:** $50,000

---

## Case Overview

**Victim:** Nancy Guthrie  
**Age:** 84 years old  
**Description:** White female, 5'4" - 5'5" tall, 150 lbs, brown hair, blue eyes  
**Last Seen:** January 31, 2026, approximately 9:50 PM  
**Location:** Catalina Foothills, Tucson, Arizona  
**Reported Missing:** February 1, 2026 at approximately 12:03 PM

Nancy Guthrie is the mother of NBC "Today" show co-anchor Savannah Guthrie. She is considered a vulnerable adult with limited mobility, has a pacemaker, and requires daily medication for a heart condition that could be fatal if not taken within 24 hours.

## Evidence and Crime Scene Details

### Physical Evidence
- **Blood on porch:** Confirmed via DNA testing to belong to Nancy Guthrie
- **Doorbell camera:** Disconnected at 1:47 AM; camera is missing from front of house
- **No video footage:** Nancy did not have active subscription, so footage was not saved
- **Personal belongings:** Purse, wallet, cell phone, and vehicle left at home
- **Medications:** Still inside the home

### Key Observations
- Sheriff stated Nancy "did not leave on her own" based on scene evidence
- Limited mobility - "could not walk 50 yards by herself"
- No cognitive issues reported
- No cameras around property were smashed or destroyed

## Ransom Notes / Communications

### Alleged Ransom Notes (Under Investigation)
Multiple ransom notes have been sent to media outlets. The FBI is investigating their authenticity.

1. **First Note (Feb 2)** - Sent to KOLD-TV and TMZ
   - Demanded payment in Bitcoin
   - Deadline: 5 PM Thursday, February 5
   - Second deadline: Monday, February 9
   - Referenced an Apple Watch and floodlight
   - Stated "you will have no way of contacting me"
   - TMZ's Harvey Levin reported note said Nancy is "OK, but scared"

2. **Second Message (Feb 6)** - Sent to KOLD-TV
   - Different IP address but same type of secure server
   - Did not make new ransom demands
   - Did not provide proof of life
   - Claimed to be from abductors

### Imposter Arrest
**Derrick Callella**, 42, of Hawthorne, California was arrested February 5, 2026 for sending fake ransom texts to the Guthrie family.

- Sent messages to Annie Guthrie and Tommaso Cioni
- Message read: "Did you get the bitcoin were [sic] waiting on our end for the transaction"
- Admitted to sending messages, said he was "just trying to see if the family would respond"
- Charges: Transmitting a demand for ransom in interstate commerce; utilizing a telecommunications device with intent to harass
- Released on $20,000 bond with conditions
- Next court appearance: February 12, 2026 in Arizona

## Suspects and Persons of Interest

### Official Statement
**No suspects or persons of interest have been officially identified** as of February 6, 2026.

Sheriff Nanos stated: "Nobody's eliminated, but we just really don't have enough to say, 'This is our suspect, this is our guy.'"

### Media Reports (Unverified)
- Former journalist Ashleigh Banfield reported that Tommaso Cioni (Nancy's son-in-law) was a "prime suspect" citing a law enforcement source
- Sheriff Nanos called this report "reckless" and said it "could actually be doing some damage to the case"
- Annie Guthrie's car was towed and impounded as part of "standard investigative practices"

### Expert Analysis
Former FBI agent Gregory Vecchi told Newsweek he's "not convinced" that law enforcement doesn't have persons of interest, suggesting they may be holding information close for operational security.
