Savannah pays tribute to husband; YouTuber arrested for 2nd time outside Nancy's home

🚨 Day 129: Former Detective Fears Nancy May Not Have Survived

Ex-detective Jon Buehler: "I'm fearful she didn't survive the abduction"

Former detective Jon Buehler told NewsNation's Brian Entin he fears the worst outcome. Two key factors: the complete lack of communication from any kidnapper since the initial period, and the nature of evidence found at the scene. "The reason I'm fearful she didn't survive the abduction is kind of twofold," he explained. The case has now reached 4+ months with no suspect identified.

Jun 8, 2026

🚨 Day 128: Savannah Opens Up About Returning to Work

"I know people wonder how I'm able to do this job... No, never. Never."

Savannah Guthrie guest co-hosted the 4th hour of Today with Jenna Bush Hager, speaking candidly about her return. "It's hard when you're with your best friend to not be real," she said. "I know maybe people wonder like, 'What's going on? How is she able to do that job? Is she not thinking about it? Did she forget?' No, never. Never." She added: "It is [a respite]. And you are my family... my mom would've said the same, like, 'Honey, just keep going, just keep going.' And so, I am."

May 29, 2026

🚨 Day 118: Ex-FBI Agent: Suspect Names Likely Among 50,000+ Tips

"I truly believe the names of the individuals involved are most likely in those tips"

Former FBI special agent Harry Trombitas told Yahoo News that over 50,000 tips have been received in the case. "I truly believe the names of the individuals involved are most likely in those tips," he said. "It's just a matter of working through them. [Investigators] may already be on to somebody right now." He noted law enforcement can't just arrest someone — "You have to build a case and think about future prosecution."

May 14, 2026

🚨 Day 103: Sheriff Confirms Evidence Withheld to Protect Case

"Yes, absolutely there are" evidence being withheld — "to protect our case, not to keep it secret"

Sheriff Chris Nanos confirmed to KOLD-TV that evidence is being deliberately withheld from the public. "But it's not done because we got to keep it secret... It's done because we got to protect our case." DNA evidence was sent to the FBI lab in Quantico, Virginia after first going to a private lab in Florida. Nanos firmly rejected the idea the case is cold: "There's way too much work to be done... We're not going to give up on it just because it's been 100 days."

May 11, 2026

🚨 Day 100: FBI Director Claims FBI Was "Kept Out" for 4 Days

Kash Patel on Hannity podcast: "The first 48 hours... we were kept out"

FBI Director Kash Patel told Sean Hannity's podcast that the FBI was excluded from the investigation for four critical days. "The first 48 hours of anyone's disappearance is critical," he said. "We were kept out of the investigation." Patel said when finally allowed in, the FBI retrieved Ring doorbell footage and called Google leadership about cached data. He added the FBI offered to send DNA to Quantico for analysis "within days" but was denied. The Pima County Sheriff's Department pushed back, stating "a member of the FBI Task Force was also notified and present at that scene."

May 10, 2026

🚨 Day 100: Sheriff Nanos — "We Will Make an Arrest"

"I believe, at some point in time, we will make an arrest on this case"

As the case reached its 100-day mark, Sheriff Chris Nanos told KOLD-TV he remains confident: "I believe, at some point in time, we will make an arrest on this case. And whoever that individual is, that individual will have a right to a fair and impartial trial." He emphasized the FBI has been involved "day one" and "every single day." The case has now surpassed 100 days with no suspect publicly named.

Apr 25, 2026

🚨 Day 85: FBI Profiler — Nancy Coughed Up Blood, Fought Back

Retired FBI profiler Jim Clemente analyzes blood evidence frame by frame

On NewsNation, retired FBI profiler Jim Clemente provided a detailed analysis of blood evidence on Nancy's porch. Medium-velocity blood spatter indicates Nancy aspirated and coughed up blood while on her knees or the ground, face within a foot of the surface. "Where that blood pattern disappears, I believe she was likely picked up and carried the rest of the way, perhaps with her face up so that there was no more blood deposited on that walkway." Clemente believes there was only ONE suspect: "If there were two people, you would think one of them would have control of her... but here, she's clearly on the ground coughing this blood up."

Apr 24, 2026

🚨 Day 84: Suspect Likely Changing Appearance; Callella Trial Set

FBI profiler: Suspect "doing what he can to change his appearance"; Fake ransom texter trial June 23

Retired FBI profiler Jim Clemente told NewsNation the suspect is likely changing his appearance, altering his car, and creating distance from the crime scene. "He's absolutely watching every bit of coverage that there is on this case." Meanwhile, Derrick Callella — the California man accused of sending fake Bitcoin ransom texts to the family — has a federal trial date set for June 23, 2026 in Tucson.

Apr 22, 2026

🚨 Day 81: Sheriff Denies New Person of Interest; Enhanced DNA Tech Deployed

"Nope." — Sheriff Nanos shuts down social media rumor in one word

Sheriff Chris Nanos shut down social media rumors of a new person of interest being detained with a one-word response to Fox News: "Nope." The rumor had spread across X and aggregator sites drawing tens of thousands of views. 🚨 Day 32: Glove Lead Traced to Restaurant Worker — Cleared

Gloves found near scene traced to nearby worker; "Has nothing to do with the case"

The black gloves found approximately two miles from Nancy's home were traced to a nearby restaurant worker. Sheriff Nanos confirmed the individual "has nothing to do with the case" and has been cleared. DNA from the gloves had already returned no matches in the FBI's CODIS database.

Feb 24, 2026 (Evening)

🚨 Sheriff: Doorbell Image Speculation "Purely Speculative"

PCSD debunks claims that photos show suspect on different days

The Pima County Sheriff's Department issued a statement Monday evening addressing speculation about the doorbell camera images. While sources told CNN and ABC News that the masked suspect appears in photos from two different days (with and without backpack), the Sheriff's Department stated: "There is no date or time stamp associated with these images. Therefore, any suggestion that the photographs were taken on different days is purely speculative." The department emphasized that "conclusions will be guided by verifiable evidence and established facts."

Feb 24, 2026

🚨 Day 24: Suspect May Have Visited Home BEFORE Feb 1

ABC News: Masked man seen at door on day PRIOR to abduction

Sources familiar with the investigation told ABC News that the masked man suspected in Nancy's abduction appears to have been at her front door on a day BEFORE February 1. The FBI image of the suspect WITHOUT a backpack was captured by her Nest doorbell camera on an earlier date. One theory: the suspect noticed the camera and was scared off, only to return later with branches to cover the lens. This explains why investigators asked neighbors to check security footage going back to January 1.

Law enforcement continues to process the 40,000-50,000 leads received and pursue new investigative avenues including Investigative Genetic Genealogy (IGG).

🚨 DNA Testing: Genetic Genealogy Now Being Used

Investigators turn to advanced DNA techniques after CODIS yields no matches

After DNA from gloves found 2 miles from Nancy's home returned no matches in the FBI's CODIS database, investigators are now using Investigative Genetic Genealogy (IGG) to analyze DNA from the gloves AND additional DNA samples found inside her residence. Sheriff Nanos emphasized the home DNA is "more critical than any glove found 2 miles away." The process could take weeks.
