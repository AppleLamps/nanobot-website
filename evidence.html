EVIDENCE & INVESTIGATIVE LOG

Biological Evidence

Item: Blood Sample (Porch)

Status: CONFIRMED — Nancy Guthrie's DNA

Details: DNA testing confirmed blood on the porch belongs to Nancy. Retired FBI profiler Jim Clemente analyzed the blood pattern (frame by frame): medium-velocity spatter indicates Nancy was on her knees or ground, face within 1 foot of surface, and coughed up blood. Three circular hollow spots show blood expelled in different directions. Pattern disappearance suggests she was picked up and carried face-up. Likely struck in nose or mouth during struggle. Source: NewsNation, April 2026

Biological Evidence

Item: Mixed DNA from Residence

Status: Under Analysis at FBI Quantico Lab

Details: Mixed DNA samples recovered from Nancy's home, including a hair sample that does NOT belong to Nancy or close contacts. DNA was first sent to a private lab in Florida, then transferred to the FBI Laboratory in Quantico, Virginia for enhanced analysis. Sheriff Nanos: "We want to make sure that DNA doesn't just identify a suspect. It also exonerates those who are innocent." Source: KOLD-TV, CBS News, May-June 2026

Physical Evidence

Item: Black Gloves Found 2 Miles from Scene

Status: NO MATCH in CODIS or FBI Database

Details: Gloves appeared to match those worn by suspect in doorbell footage. DNA from gloves yielded NO matches in FBI's CODIS database or DNA found on Nancy's property. In March 2026, gloves were traced to a nearby restaurant worker who was cleared: "has nothing to do with the case." Investigative Genetic Genealogy (IGG) was attempted but no matches found. Source: Pima County Sheriff, March 2026

Suspect Description

Item: Doorbell Camera Footage

Status: Suspect Unidentified

Details: FBI-released images show masked male, 5'9"-5'10", average build. Armed with handgun. Wearing gloves. Carrying black 25-liter Ozark Trail Hiker Pack backpack. Red-soled boots visible. Possible visible wrist tattoo. Suspect attempted to BLOCK the Nest camera (not destroy it) — former FBI agent Michael Harrigan described this as "amateurish." Footage captured from residual backend data after Google/FBI retrieved cached data. Source: FBI, NewsNation, April 2026

Digital Evidence

Item: Doorbell Camera & Software

Status: Disconnected at 1:47 AM / Motion at 2:12 AM

Details: Camera disconnected at 1:47 AM. Motion detected at 2:12 AM but no video saved due to lack of active subscription. FBI Director Kash Patel personally called Google leadership to request cached data from before deletion. FBI recovered still images from backend data. Former FBI profiler: suspect's attempt to block rather than destroy camera was a critical mistake. Source: FBI, Hannity Podcast (Kash Patel), May 2026

Digital Evidence

Item: Pacemaker Data

Status: Disconnected @ 2:28 AM (Feb 1)

Details: Critical data point. Pacemaker disconnected from monitoring app on Nancy's phone, which remained at the house. FBI deployed "signal sniffers" (Bluetooth detection technology) from helicopters to locate device. Range: 10-30 meters. No confirmed signal detected in Tucson desert searches.

Physical Evidence

Item: Ransom Communications

Status: Multiple Sources / Mostly Fake

Details: 1. KOLD 13 Note: Included specific details (Apple Watch placement, floodlight).
2. Bitcoin Demand: Multimillion-dollar demand to media outlets.
3. Second Message (Feb 6): Received by KOLD 13. No ransom demand. Sent from different IP but similarly encrypted. Contains "insider" details.
4. Imposter Texts — Derrick Callella: California man (42) arrested Feb 5 for sending fake Bitcoin ransom texts to Annie Guthrie and Tommaso Cioni on Feb 4. Used VOIP to disguise number. Traced to his Gmail and Hawthorne, CA home. Admitted sending messages. Released on $20,000 bond. Federal trial scheduled June 23, 2026 in Tucson. NOT linked to original ransom demands.
Source: FBI, KGUN, JubileeCast

Physical Evidence

Item: Medication

Status: Left at Home

Details: Nancy's essential blood pressure and heart medication was left behind. Pacemaker-dependent. Missing doses can be life-threatening.

Expert Analysis

Analyst: Jim Clemente, Retired FBI Profiler

Key Findings:

• Single Suspect Theory: "If there were two people, one would have control of her... but here, she's clearly on the ground coughing this blood up."
• Nancy Fought Back: Blood pattern shows she was assaulted at front door, likely struck in nose/mouth, fell to knees, aspirated blood.
• Suspect Mistakes: Blocking camera instead of destroying it was "amateurish." Suspect is "not a sophisticated criminal... bumbling his way through this."
• Suspect Mindset (April 2026): Likely changing appearance, altering car, creating distance from crime scene, setting up alibis, "watching every bit of coverage."
• Prediction: "The suspect made mistakes. Those mistakes will directly lead to his capture."
Source: NewsNation (Brian Entin podcast), Fox News, OK! Magazine, April-May 2026
